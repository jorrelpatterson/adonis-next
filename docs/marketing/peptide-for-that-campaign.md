# "There's a peptide for that" — Campaign Library

**Status:** Active campaign · IG-first · launches 2026-05-15

A long-running Instagram series riffing on Apple's "There's an app for that." Each post poses a relatable male-optimization gripe in 2-4 layman words, then reveals the peptide compound research has investigated for that area.

---

## Concept

| Element | Approach |
|---|---|
| **Hook tone** | Layman / kitchen-table. Real guys say "knees hurt" not "joint inflammation." 2-4 words max. |
| **Format** | 2-slide carousel, 1080×1080 each. Slide 1 = hook (deep navy bg). Slide 2 = research reveal (cream bg). |
| **Voice shift** | Hook = colloquial, brand-warm. Body / slide 2 / caption = research-grade editorial. The contrast is the strategy. |
| **RUO compliance** | Eyebrow + footer always frame as "what the literature is looking at." No outcome claims, no therapeutic implications, no "you will…" Always: *"What you do with the research is up to you and your clinician."* |
| **Cadence** | Mon · Wed · Fri at 3 posts/week. Campaign hooks and routine deep-dives are interleaved throughout — no two posts about the same compound within 3 slots, and categories (recovery, cognitive, sleep, longevity, sexual, etc.) rotate each post. Greedy-balanced 2026-04-28. Runs 2026-05-04 → 2026-10-05. |
| **Mod-risk handling** | Sexual-health, GH, IGF, AICAR, Tesamorelin posts use stricter framing. Weight-loss compounds skipped on IG entirely (email-only). |

## Image filename convention

`ptt-[slug]-[slide].png`

- `ptt-` = "There's a peptide for that" prefix
- `[slug]` = lowercase, hyphenated hook (e.g. `dead-bedroom`, `knees-hurt`, `wake-up-tired`)
- `[slide]` = `1` for hook, `2` for reveal

Examples:
- `ptt-dead-bedroom-1.png`, `ptt-dead-bedroom-2.png`
- `ptt-knees-hurt-1.png`, `ptt-knees-hurt-2.png`

## Mod-risk-specific framing notes

**Sexual-health (PT-141, Kisspeptin):** Avoid `#bremelanotide`. Lean on neural-mechanism language ("MC3R/MC4R central pathways"). No direct sexual-outcome claims in caption.

**GH / Sermorelin / IGF / Tesamorelin:** Avoid `#testosterone`, `#trt`, `#hrt`. Use `#hormoneresearch`, `#endocrinepeptides`, `#peptidescience`. Frame as HPG/GH research, never as "low-T treatment."

**AICAR:** WADA-banned for sport. Frame as "exercise mimetic research" — never link to athletic enhancement directly. No sport-specific tags.

**Weight-loss compounds:** Skip IG entirely. Email-only.
