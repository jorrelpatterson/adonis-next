How to read this. All draw amounts are in insulin-syringe units on a standard U-100 (1 mL / 100-unit) syringe — 10 units = 0.1 mL. "Reconstitute" means adding the listed bacteriostatic (BAC) water to the powder. These vials hold up to ~3 mL; every recipe below stays at 1–2 mL to leave headroom. Aim water at the glass wall, swirl gently — never shake. Once mixed, refrigerate and use within ~28 days.

+ Injection & Storage

SubQ injection — step by step

  1. Wash hands. Wipe the vial top and the skin site with an alcohol pad.
  2. Draw your dose. Flip the vial, pull to the unit mark, tap out air bubbles, push air back out.
  3. Pinch skin at the site, insert at 45°, push the plunger slowly and steadily.
  4. Remove, light pressure with the pad. New needle every injection.
  5. Rotate sites: abdomen (2″ from navel), outer thigh, love handles, back of upper arm.

Storage & handling

  • Powder (sealed): room temp or fridge, away from heat/light — stable for months.
  • After mixing: refrigerate (36–46 °F), use within ~28 days.
  • BAC water: store at room temperature; it keeps the vial sterile between draws.
  • Never freeze reconstituted compounds — freezing destroys them.
  • Solution should be clear. Discard if cloudy or discolored.
